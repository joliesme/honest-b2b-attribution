# Vocabulary

Attribution fights are usually vocabulary fights. Sales doesn't dispute your data; sales disputes your verb.

## The tiers of claim strength

1. **Campaign-Touched** (use this): the account or lead has verified exposure to campaign activity within the defined window. Provable from platform and CRM data.
2. **Campaign-Influenced** (use with care): touched, plus subsequent engagement (content download, event attendance, reply). Defensible if the engagement definition is written down.
3. **Campaign-Sourced** (almost never): the campaign was demonstrably the first touch that created the record. In enterprise B2B, buying committees and dark-social research make true first touch mostly unknowable. Claiming it invites the one counterexample that discredits the whole report.

## Rules

- Pick the strongest claim you can prove, then use it consistently. Mixed vocabulary in one dashboard reads as motivated reasoning.
- Define the influence window explicitly (e.g. exposure within 90 days before MQL date) and print it on the dashboard.
- Never let a deck reword your labels upstream. "Campaign-Touched pipeline" becoming "marketing-generated pipeline" in the leadership summary is how methodology dies.

## The disclaimer

Every report carries a short methodology note beside the headline number, not in an appendix. Mine reads roughly: "Campaign-Touched means the account had verified campaign exposure in the stated window. This measures association, not causation." Publishing your limitations first is what earns the right to be believed on everything else.
